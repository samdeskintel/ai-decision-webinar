Setting the scene
Kris
The three failure modes
Security operations are fragmented — tools solve parts of the problem, not the whole
Detection & alerting tools
Tools like Dataminr automate signal detection. But they surface alerts — not decisions. Analysts still spend hours on context, validation, and org impact. Signal volume without decisioning intelligence.
Good at detection — weak at decision
Mass comms & specialist tools
Everbridge, Ontic, ESRI and others solve specific problems — notifications, investigations, GIS. They have some alerting, but it's not their core strength. They don't connect detection to decision.
Good at delivery — blind at decision
The gap between tools
The real cost isn't any single tool — it's the human work required to connect them. Validating alerts, building context, mapping org impact, deciding what to do. That work lives between the tools.
The decision layer is missing
Most teams have tools. What they don't have is a connected operational cycle — from early warning through to resolution — with AI compressing every stage.
